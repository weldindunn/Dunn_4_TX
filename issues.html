ISSUES

Climate Change

In the past 5 years, Houston has seen just about every natural hazard the world has to throw at us. Even though we always make it through stronger than ever, it has become abundantly clear that these disasters will only get worse with the threat of climate change, whether that be flooding, hurricanes, aggressive snow storms, or extreme heat. I'm not anti-oil by any means. My own father was an oiler, and without oil Houston wouldn't be were it is today. But with great power comes great responsibility. I have called on the rest of congress and the president to take grand action against climate change. We need green, high-tech solutions for a better Houston, a better Texas, and a better America.

Voting Rights

Ultimately everything comes down to voting. Without the say of the people in our government, it has no purpose. In recent years, especially post-2020, we have seen serious attacks on the people's right to vote. Right here in Texas we have seen gross laws both proposed and passed to restrict people from having their voice heard. Many of my colleagues and I recognize that it is critical to take action against these threats and assure all qualified Americans have the ability to vote in a safe and secure manner. I support reforms to make election day a federal holiday and to guarantee the right to voting by mail.

Houston on the World Stage

In my lifetime, Houston has gone from "that place where the Oilers play" to the fourth biggest city in the United States. It has become one of the most important trade hubs in the country. In 2021, the Port of Houston alone accounted for over $40 billion in exports. We have become a serious player on the world stage, and it is key for us to have someone in congress to stand up for what is right in the world. As we re-build our relations that have been damaged by previous administrations and re-establish ourselves as a trustable and repsonsible leader on the world stage, Houston will stand out as the city on a hill and no doubt be a model for how to build business, innovation, and culture. I support trade agreements like the TPP that benefit Houstonians and Americans, as well as other developing nations around the world that may otherwise fall under the influence of China.

As a veteran of the US Air Force and former astronaut, it is critical that we maintain positive military relations with the world and stand by our allies. Whether it be Ukraine, the Kurds, or Israel, we must show that we will support our friends without starting unnecessary conflicts like the ones we have seen all too often.
